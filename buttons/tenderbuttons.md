 BUTTONS.
A button is what a button when a button is.
Not the hole, not the hold, not the fold.
A button is a circle with its own circle.
Pressed, pressed again, not pressure, only place.

Silken button, wooden button, chrome button in the chrome of no cloth.
More than four holes. Less than one.
A hole may be a button. A button may not be a hole.

One button is not alone if the next is aligned.
Two buttons are a system.
Three buttons are an alphabet.

In browsers there are buttons with back in them.
A back is not a front.
Refresh is not a rinse.
Stop is a square pretending to be a door.

Buttoned-up, buttoned-down.
Nothing stays unless it's fastened gently.
Nothing opens unless it forgets how it closed.

A button is a maybe with a loop.
A button is not because but before.
To button is not to close but to guess.

So buttons.
So many buttons.
So buttoning




PIXEL.
A pixel is not a dot.
A dot is not enough.
A pixel is maybe the smallest decision.

Each pixel is a vote for presence.
Pixels do not touch but they imply touching.

Red pixel.
Green pixel.
Blue pixel.
Together they lie.

You never see the pixel.
You see the chorus.
